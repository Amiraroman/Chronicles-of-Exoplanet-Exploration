What is an Exoplanet?

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. Scientists have discovered thousands of them, many of which are located in the habitable zone where liquid water could exist.

Missions & Exploration

Several space telescopes such as Kepler, TESS, and the James Webb Space Telescope are designed to discover and study exoplanets.

Major Discoveries

Explore some of the most exciting exoplanets discovered so far, including Kepler-452b, a super-Earth located in the habitable zone of its star.

Future of Exploration

Upcoming missions will focus on directly imaging exoplanets and searching for biosignatures that might indicate the presence of life.

Scientists discover planet orbiting closest single star to our Sun

Using the European Southern Observatory’s Very Large Telescope (ESO’s VLT), astronomers have discovered an exoplanet orbiting Barnard’s star, the closest single star to our Sun. On this newly discovered exoplanet, which has at least half the mass of Venus, a year lasts just over three Earth days.
